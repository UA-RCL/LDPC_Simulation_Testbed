Motivation and Impact

Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are a popular choice for employment in several cutting edge communication standards such as 5G, WiFi, WiMax, 10 Gigabit Ethernet etc.. With the increasing popularity of LDPC codes, the research to obtain decoders with better error correction performance and reduced hardware footprint is becoming prominent as well. One crucial aspect of this research is the characterization of novel decoders through high resolution simulation. The recent improvements in decoders have raised the scale of this simulation to upto 1014. The feasibility of the time scale for completing such simulations on general purpose CPU based software platforms is becoming a concern.

This project presents an FPGA based high throughput and modular simulation testbed for rapid simulation of decoders for LDPC codes. The researchers can replace different modules of this testbed to incorporate their own hard-decision decoders for rapid characterization.

The presented simulation testbed reduces the simulation duration for Probabilistic Gallger B (PGaB) decoder, down to 24 hours from an estimated 199 years on a single core CPU platform.
